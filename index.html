How It Actually Works

Unlike AI detection tools that try to spot fakes, Checkmark provides cryptographic proof of authenticity by verifying the photo at the moment of capture.

1

Device Authentication

When you take a photo, Checkmark verifies your device's unique hardware signature using the device's Trusted Execution Environment (TEE) or Secure Enclave. This confirms the photo is coming from a real, uncompromised camera.

2

Timestamp & Metadata Capture

We record the precise timestamp, GPS coordinates, camera settings, and device information at the moment of capture. This metadata is cryptographically signed and cannot be altered without detection.

3

Camera Verification

We verify the camera isn't spoofed by checking hardware attestation certificates from the device manufacturer. This ensures the photo came from an authentic camera sensor, not a virtual camera or screen recording.

4

Cryptographic Signing

The photo and its metadata are combined and signed using public-key cryptography. This creates an unforgeable mathematical proof linking the image to its capture moment and device.

5

Blockchain Recording

The signature and verification code are permanently recorded on a public blockchain. This creates an immutable, timestamped record that anyone can verify independently.

6

Public Verification

Anyone can enter your verification code on our website to confirm the photo's authenticity. If you make it public, they can also view the original verified image and all its metadata.

The Problem

In 2026, AI can generate photorealistic images indistinguishable from reality. Anyone can edit photos seamlessly. There's no way to prove what's real. Journalists can't verify sources. Courts can't trust evidence. Social media is flooded with convincing fakes. Trust in visual media has collapsed.

Why Not AI Detection?

AI detection tools try to spot fakes by looking for artifacts and patterns. But they're playing catch-up‚Äîevery time detection improves, generation gets better. They produce false positives and false negatives. They can't prove a photo is real, only guess whether it might be fake. We need proof, not probability.

Our Approach

Checkmark doesn't try to detect fakes. Instead, we provide cryptographic proof that a photo is authentic by verifying it at capture. Using device signatures, hardware attestation, and blockchain recording, we create unforgeable evidence that a photo is real. It's impossible to fake because the proof is mathematical, not algorithmic.
